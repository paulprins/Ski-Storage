This is the ski that I'm currently using. I got it for when I go racing. This is the best ski that I've ever owned. I love it. It works well at high speeds and floats pretty well in the pow. I ski on the 173 (98-99 model) and it's also great in the park. It isn't to good in the bumps because it's go a side cut and looks just ok. But I would recommend this ski to someone any day.

Rating 4 of 5

The can carve very well. Handle ice good and are just like most all mountain skis. But back to the park. They spin good. Look cool. They are kinda heavy. Line
1260
www.lineski.com

Paul Prins (Freeski520@aol.com),
SkiStorage
Minnesota - Hyland Hills

I'll tell you that these are the best skis for the park. Yes even better than the 1080. They are symmetrical and center mounted. both of them have there advantages. The center mount makes spinning really easy. They are lighter than the 10's probably just because they are shorter. When you first put them on they feel really short because there is almost no ski in front of the boot compared to what I'm used to. They are super easy to land switch on and that also means easy to ski switch (easier on tens). But over all I would say that they are a better ski. and for the $315 they are worth it. But out of the park. THEY SUCK. These are a pipe/park only ski. They are hard to get an edge in. They are really flimsy at higher speeds. But if your a park bum and that's all you would use them for. Get them. (note mine did break the first day on them. But the said it was bad glue and it makes sence. It wouldn't keep me from them)

Rating 3 (5 if park bum) of 5
