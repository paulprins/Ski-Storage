The Good Old Days

Part 1

Bob Lacy(jyarddog@spiritone.com),
Oregon - Mt.Hood Area

       Well, Sonny! Back when I was your age we alked to school... 5 miles, uphill, both ways... in 5 ft of snow... in the heat of July! Oh! sorry! Forgot to take my Viagra and prozac! bwahahaha
      Winter of 56-57, 5th grade. I didn't give a rip about girls, just skiing, or learning how to. (I had it bad!) I had a fever the night before. This was because I was so excited in going. Mom said I wasn't going if the fever wasn't down by morning. You never saw a kid hit the sack so fast! The next day I was on my way to Mt. Hood Ski Bowl to learn how to ski. A bus load of screaming kids arrives and we all pile out to the back of the bus to get our skis, which are all piled on top of each other. Solid wood skis long enough to reach from the ground to the first knuckle on your longest finger, standing straight with your arm stretched skyward as high as possible. Some of us had the newer metal poles. Some had the standard bamboo poles. The poles had metal rings for baskets which were attached by leather straps to the pole itself. All skis by this time had metal edges.Most came in sections so if a part of the edge got hit or bent you could buy another section (8" or so) and screw it in place. When metal edges first came on the scene it was considered cheating and unfair advantage. Sounds like some guys today. (The more things change, the more they remain the same).
       I had bear claw bindings and was told to get new bindings fast. I got something that looked like a flat, but wavy piece of metal with a dimple in it and a metal piece with a bump on it which was screwed into the toe of my boots. This fit into the dimple. We all still had the cable which you hooked around a groove in the heel of your boot, and then you threw the lever forward or backward, which ever the lever action was for that binding. You had metal cable guides along the sides of your skis for the cable! Some of us even repositioned the farthest back guide further back in order to get a more downward pull on the heel  to keep the heel from lifting up so damned much. One girl in our class had one of the new safety bindings from Marker, "SkiFree".
      Boots were great. About 350 different makers. Some even had an inner boot also made of leather. Us cool dudes made neat lacing patterns while lacing up our boots. This took place about 20 minutes before you got to the ski area. End of the day always saw your feet soaking wet. That's why you always brought an extra pair of socks and a towel. Your boots always went back into the boot tree, if you could con your folks into buying one for you. This would clamp down on your boot with wingnuts to keep the toe from curling up like an elve's sandal!!!
Instructors seemed to have the best stuff. SkiFree bindings or the like with long leather straps which you wrapped around your boots which ended up in a clamp type buckle. This looked cool and important! The rest of us had nothing. When your ski came off in a fall-which rarely happened... (yeah, right), The ski would take off downhill seeming to scream, "I'm free! I'm free! Long live liberty!" When it happened to someone else it was always a good excuse to yell,"Runnaway ski!" And we all would take off after it. It was a good excuse to go hell bent for election downhill after it and go as fast as we could. Of course we didn't have a snowball's chance in hell catching the damned thing, but it was a good excuse to go fast. This tended to piss off the ski patrol who warned us many times to stop it or he'd pull our tags! (You just had to watch out for thsoe ski patrol guys. If you didn't see any.... Go for it!
     At Ski Bowl between upper bowl and lower there was a small hut where you could get lunch and get warm. Today it is an historical landmark (makes ya feel old!) They call it a warming hut. No burgers, no fries, just fancy foo-foo drinks like Italian ices, beer, chips. Yuppie food! Yechhhh!
     Chair lifts were great! Wooden towers, single chairs, got caught on one for 2 1/2 hrs. during a storm once. Upper bowl had a double chair! But none of us was good enough to do the upper bowl. it's so steep it is called the "Back Wall". That's it for now. More stabs from the past for another time. Bob
