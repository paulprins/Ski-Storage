Resorts

Snowbird
www.snowbird.com

Paul Prins (Freeski520@aol.com),
SkiStorage
Minnesota - Hyland Hills

Snow Bird is my favorite resort. I'll tell you why. The place is just awesome. They have the best powder that I have ever skied in my whole life and lots of it (About 500 inches). It is as steep as you could ever want with huge cliffs and natural kickers all over. I hit this one kicker and got launched at least 40 or 50 feet. I didn't get a chance to go i the pipe or the Terrain Park last season so I couldn't tell you what those are like. Last year I hooked up with some snowboarders who knew the place front and back so that really helped. This season they open up the back side and it looks very promising. So if you have the chance head out to utah and ski the bird.

Rating 4.75 of 5

Alta
www.altaskiarea.com

Paul Prins (Freeski520@aol.com),
SkiStorage
Minnesota - Hyland Hills

Alta is just up the cannon from Snow Bird. It gets a little more snow every year than snow bird. About 500 inches. The place is huge. I still haven't skied the whole thing. And it is pure old school. No high speed lifts, No terrain park, No halfpipe, Just good old fashion skiing. If your in the mood for untracked powder head over here. With a little looking you can find some fresh stuff. It's tons of fun and there are a lot of cliffs to drop off of so be ready.

Rating 4 of 5

Timberline Lodge

Bob Lacy(jyarddog@spiritone.com),
Oregon - Mt.Hood Area

Built during the depression in the 30's. Dedicated 1937 by Roosevelt on the same day he dedicated Bonneville Dam. (busy guy that day!) It was a CCC and WPA I think project to get people working again. There were a lot of voluteers too. Beautiful lodge!&nbsp; Skiing- Puche (pronounced 'poochie) a blue run with a couple of off shoots of short blacks and a green. Victoria Station- nice long run I would call a high level blue with a couple of short black off shoots back to Puche. You can kick up some decent speed on this one. Not too many people (kiddies) on it. Puche you gotta watch it. kiddies, classes and boarders sitting all over the place. Magic Mile- mile long run. lower level blue, very wide. Very wide- can go as fast or as slow as you want. Good place to take beginners and inters. Silcox Lodge is at the top of Magic Mile. Good place to have lunch. Yuo can reserve the place for weddings and even stay there but the waiting list is long. It's an historic site. Small, rustic, and gorgeous inside... right out of the 30's! Palmer- higher up than the Mile. Some call it a low level black. I call it a high level blue.

Mt. Hood Meadows

Oregon - Mt.Hood Area

Mt. Hood Meadows- good place to ski. good runs. good moguls in places.One face always is full of moguls. Many moons ago I took the face aftera season of advanced lessons. My timing was perfect! I was looking likethe guys in the videos and movies! I remember my brain starting to pumpmy ego. That's when I lost it; not paying attention to looking ahead. hit the back side of a mogul, not paying attention to my line, double flip, landed on my back, yard sale time! The lift close by had stopped and all the people I noticed were leaning forward trying to find out if I was ok. So without getting up I lift my hands and arms to the sky and yell, "God! Why me?" Everyone laughed.

Ski Bowl

Bob Lacy(jyarddog@spiritone.com),
Oregon - Mt.Hood Area

First ski area on the mountain. Upper bowl and lower bowl and outback. I haven't skied the outback. My skis are too thin. I'd sink like a rock. Upper bowl was the steepest place in the world to ski (commercially developed) in 1937 or so. Short run but nice. Also the largest night skiing area in the nation. At the top of the upper bowl you can take beginners around the ridge on a green run! This way they can actually see what a black looks like going up the lift, without having to take the black run. Ski Bowl is lower on the mt. so the snow is wetter sometimes.
