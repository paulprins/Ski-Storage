1999 New Zealand Events
Another fantastic season of Southern Hemisphere riding culminated on the 2nd of October, at both the Cardrona and Treble Cone (TC) ski fields. This season saw a number of firsts at TC including, a skier/boarder Slopestyle on the 6th of August and a skier/boarder Supercross on the 7th. These events were unique in that it was the first time that the two disciplines were run during the same event at TC.

August 6th Slope Style

The Slopestyle was a huge success! With a total of 25 athletes competing and ranging in ability from young beginners to world class riders set the stage for a fun filled day of intense competition combined with a strong sense of camaraderie. A large Japanese contingency was a major factor in making the event a success. After registration the riders were given 45 minutes to familiarize themselves with the "hits" and then New Zealand Freeskier Camp coaches Josh Loubek and Marc McDonnell foreran the course throwing a slew of rodeo 720's, misty flips, mute grabs, and impressive switch back flips. The crowd of about 75 which had already gathered to listen to a local DJ spin high powered hip-hop and techno swelled to about a 125 after seeing the 2 pro's huck their meat. The location of the event was ideal for competitor and spectator alike as it was in close proximity to TC's 6-person chair, which allowed one to enjoy the other. Once all was fastened, buckled, tightened, and greased, the boarders were run first. To simplify the judging criterion and still keep an equitable system flags were used to determine which competitor advanced. The 3 judges each had a blue and a red flag and competitors were run in a dual format judged on technical merit, difficulty, and amplitude.

In the skier pool, which had just as many competitors, the Japanese ruled. With the likes of up and coming stars Taisuke Kusunoki and Yuki Kadota making it to the finals, the last dual of the day was huge!!! Taisuke, a 15-year old school boy from Japan, was throwing huge 720 grabs and going the biggest anyone went all season. Yuki, who also competed in the Pulsate Wanaka Big Air, flawlessly threw lincoln loops and misty 720'swith one of his huge laid out front flips to win it. Overall, the event was awesome. Judges, competitors, and spectators had a great day in the warm winter sun of New Zealand.

August 7th Supercross

August 7th saw the first combined skier/boarder Supercross in the Saddle at TC. With the help of the TC cat drivers, we were able to build quite a harrowing course. Right out of the gates were 2 huge rollers and then a sharp right turn, which was extremely effective in separating the pack. Following the start the course swooped down into one of TCs natural half pipes. The half pipe consisted of four banked turns followed by a step-up jump. After the jump there were three more banked turns to a straightaway with several rollers allowing riders to launch from one to another. Several of the skiers cleared the last two leading down into the finish. Kiwi, Sam Hazeldine, demonstrated his skiing prowess by edging out Sam Hall and Josh Loubek to take first place. Local, Lizzie Spencer won the women skiers and the men and women's boarder final was an all-Japanese final.

September 17 Big Air

Due to adverse conditions the Slopestyle, originally scheduled for the 20th of August, was re-scheduled to the 17th of September. Unfortunately, by the 17th, 2 of the 4 hits in the terrain park had become unrideable, so we decided to make the competition a Big Air. The competitors were given 4 runs with the combined score of the two best runs advancing to the final. Women boarders Pip Grant and Romy Kaiser threw some nice straight air grabs and were awarded with a pair of Fate pants and New Zealand Freeskier Camp t-shirts. The 6 men that advanced to the boarders' finals shredded.

Winner Ollie Burke said," This event went off. It was a perfect day to come out and huck in the Spring sun and snow."
